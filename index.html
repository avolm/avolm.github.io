       When I was growing up my father was in the military, so we moved around very often. By the time I was six I had lived in four homes and two apartments. My family struggled a lot, and this created a very unstable environment. But we remodeled a lot of the houses we lived in, and I think that stuck with me. Now I am majoring in interior design so I can help people create a healthy stable environment. I want to continue in my major because I love turning a house into a home, but most importantly I love helping people and I want them to thrive in their space.

     When I graduate I would love to renovate nursing homes. I would visit nursing homes a lot as a child to sing for the older people or do Christmas plays. I remember always feeling uncomfortable in nursing homes. Now that I am majoring in interior design I understand better why I felt so uncomfortable. The living conditions in most nursing homes are not ideal. My heart goes out to the elderly living in nursing homes, because many struggle with depression, anxiety, and loneliness, and I want to help these people feel like they are home. If I cannot renovate nursing homes I would love to work for a firm that did a lot of aging in place work.
